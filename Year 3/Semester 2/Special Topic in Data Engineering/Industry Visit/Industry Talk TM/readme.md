# Reflection Industry Talk TM

Data Engineering isn’t just about building pipelines — it’s about understanding how a business truly works. 💡

I recently had the opportunity to attend an insightful industry talk by Telekom Malaysia (TM), presented by Mr. Zaid Waqi Zulkifli , and it completely changed the way I view enterprise data architecture.

One thing that stood out to me is how enterprises are not just “big companies,” but complex ecosystems of interconnected business processes — from Marketing and Sales to Finance, HR, and Supply Chain. To support these operations, organizations rely on multiple enterprise systems such as CRM, ERP, and HCM platforms, each containing thousands of tables, relationships, and workflows. This is where the real challenge of Data Engineering begins.

The hardest part is often not the coding or the pipelines, but answering critical business questions:
• Which system contains the source of truth?
• Which version of the data is correct?
• How do we reconcile conflicting information across systems?

One quote from the session really stayed with me:
“The job of a data engineer is not merely moving data. The job is discovering how the business actually works.”
This talk gave me a much deeper appreciation of how data, systems, and business strategy are tightly connected in real-world enterprise environments. A huge thank you to the TM team and Zaid Waqi Zulkifli for sharing such valuable industry insights and experiences! 🚀
