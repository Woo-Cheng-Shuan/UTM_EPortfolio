## 💭 Reflection

This course provided a strong foundation in mathematical concepts that are widely applied in computer science. Through topics such as set theory, relations, functions, counting techniques, recurrence relations, graph theory, and trees, a deeper understanding of logical reasoning and problem-solving was developed.

The ability to analyze and solve computational problems using mathematical approaches was strengthened through various exercises and assignments. Concepts such as counting methods and recurrence relations helped improve analytical thinking, while graph theory and trees demonstrated how complex real-world problems can be represented and solved efficiently.

In addition, exposure was gained to finite automata and finite state machines, which introduced the fundamental concepts behind computational models and system design. These topics provided valuable insights into how abstract mathematical theories can be applied to the development of computing systems and electronic devices.

Overall, this course enhanced logical thinking and problem-solving abilities while establishing an important theoretical foundation for future computer science and data engineering studies.
