## 💭 Reflection

This course provided a comprehensive introduction to digital logic and its role as the foundation of modern computing systems. Through the study of digital circuits, Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational and sequential circuits, and programmable logic devices, a deeper understanding of how digital systems operate was developed.

Theoretical concepts were reinforced through laboratory activities, where hands-on experience was gained using simulation software, digital components, and circuit design tools. These practical sessions helped bridge the gap between classroom learning and real-world applications by demonstrating how digital circuits are designed, tested, and implemented.

In addition, valuable insights were gained into the fundamental technologies that support computers, embedded systems, industrial automation, and other microprocessor-based applications. The course also strengthened analytical and logical thinking skills through circuit analysis and problem-solving exercises.

Overall, this course established a strong foundation in digital electronics and computer hardware, providing essential knowledge that supports further studies in computer science and information technology.
